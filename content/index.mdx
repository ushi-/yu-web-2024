I'm Yosuke Ushigome, a London-based creative practitioner who straddles the lines between an experienced interaction designer, an ok-level digital prototyper, and an amateur electronics repairer. I currently work for [Normally](https://www.normally.com), occasionally teaching at [UAL Creative Computing Institute](https://www.arts.ac.uk/creative-computing-institute) and volunteering at [Repair Cafe Nunhead](https://www.instagram.com/repaircafe_nunhead/). My first name is pronounced [yōs-ké], and my pronouns are he/him ([— About](/about)). I use design, prototyping, and repair to facilitate the imagination and discussion about more just and sustainable ways we could interact with technologies. Recent projects include research and visualisation of the diverse nature of transitions towards sustainable futures for Hitachi, and developing a more cultural and spiritual interaction with AI voice assistants with Swarovski ([— Projects](/projects)). I also write and talk about the roles of design and technology in the age of crises. Like this article about the societal implications of seemingly small design decisions, and alternative interactions with technology for the transition towards sustainable and just worlds we want ([— Thoughts](/thoughts)). You can contact me at [yu@yosukeushigo.me](mailto:yu@yosukeushigo.me).
